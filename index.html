    I am an established researcher at ETH Zurich in the group of Prof. Laurent Vanbever. My current research focuses on sustainable networking, specifically in wired computer networks. In the past, I worked on low-power wireless communication protocols, (real-time) scheduling theory, and statistics applied to experimental design. You can find out more about my research interests and past activities in my resume and publication page.

    In 2019, I completed my doctoral studies under the supervision of Prof. Lothar Thiele, also at ETH. My dissertation is entitled Leveraging Synchronous Transmissions for the Design of Real-time Wireless Cyber-Physical Systems.
